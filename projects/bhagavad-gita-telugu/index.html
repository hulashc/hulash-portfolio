Why the Bhagavad Gita Endures

  • → A 5,000-year-old dialogue between Arjuna and Krishna — distilled into 700 verses on duty, action, and the self.
  • → The concept of Nishkama Karma — act without attachment to outcomes — has influenced thinkers from Thoreau to Gandhi.
  • → It frames the battlefield as a metaphor for inner conflict — the war between duty and desire every person faces.
  • → The Telugu tradition carries its own poetic lineage — scholars like Tikkana translated it into verse that resonates with South Indian philosophy.
  • → This project surfaces that wisdom live via API — making ancient text accessible and programmable for the modern web.
